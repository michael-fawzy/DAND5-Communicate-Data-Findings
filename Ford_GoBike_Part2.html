Investigation Overview¶

The overall goals of the presentation are to show the following:

- How long the average trip takes.
- When most trips are taken in terms of "month of the year" and "day of the week".
- Whethter the trip duration depends on the user type or not.
- Copmaring trip duration through months to conclude Whether the idea is succeeding or not.

Dataset Overview¶

This data set includes information about individual rides made in GoBike bike-sharing system (now called Baywheels) covering the greater San Francisco Bay area in the first 3 months of 2020.

The dataset was downloaded from https://s3.amazonaws.com/baywheels-data/index.html and multiple data files needed to be joined together to get a single dataset that required some data wrangling in order to make it tidy for analysis.

Does the trip duration depend on the user type or not?¶

At durations less than or equal to 60 minutes, both customers (casual) and subscribers (members) have achieved similar duartions of use.

When viewed by Month, without setting a duration limit, the plot shows that some customers had longer trip durations than subscribers.

Is the idea succeeding?¶

YES!

Average Trip duration has been increasing since Jan 2020 and reached it's peak in March which concludes that people are depending more on bikes to go to farther places .
